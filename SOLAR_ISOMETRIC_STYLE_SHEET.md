# Solar Isometric — Style Sheet

*A visual language for SolarPunks 26*

---

## The Name

**Solar Isometric.** Not "Solar Punk pixel art." Not "Eco-futurist character design." Solar Isometric — said like a school of work. The name carries the two non-negotiable halves: **Solar** = warmth, community, optimism, visible faces, functional beauty. **Isometric** = EBOY grid discipline, 2:1 pixel ratio, the specific technical grammar this style is built on.

If it doesn't feel Solar and it doesn't feel Isometric, it isn't this style.

---

## What It Feels Like

Imagine a Saturday morning in a city where the people who show up are people who stayed. The light is good. Someone's fixing something — not because it broke, but because maintaining shared things is what you do. There's a dog. There might be a robot dog. Nobody's hiding.

Characters look like they belong to a world that figured something out. They are not survivors. They are inheritors.

---

## Visual Principles (DO)

### 1. The Grid Is Law
- All characters use the **EBOY TiliX 7×7 Dual Pixel grid system**
- **2:1 isometric ratio** — horizontal lines go right, vertical lines go up-right at 26.565°
- No vanishing points. No forced perspective. Flat planes only.
- Characters are **exactly 15px tall** in the base grid. Head is 5-6px (33-40% of height). This is not flexible.
- Every element aligns to the grid. Nothing floats. Nothing anti-aliases.

### 2. Faces Always Visible
- This is the **Privacy Agent** rule: no masks, no helmets, no full visors obscuring the face
- Faces are warm, expressive, and grounded — not blank slates, not hyper-detailed. Blocky pixel faces read clearly at the grid scale
- Eyes, nose, mouth — or the suggestion of them. At 5px head height, you get dots, lines, or nothing. All three are fine. Blank is not.

### 3. Warmth Over Cool
- The default palette is **warm**. Teals, ambers, leaf greens, terra cotta, solar yellow. Not navy-black-cyber-grey.
- When you use a cool color (deep blues, lavender), it is an accent against warmth, not the base
- The single most important palette rule: **the dominant color must be a warm hue (HSB hue 20-80)**. Cool colors are supporting cast only.
- Exception: Tech Class may push cooler — but never pure grey. Teal counts as warm-adjacent.

### 4. Clothing Is Functional
- Jumpsuits, utility vests, cargo pants, tool belts, work boots — clothes that work
- Logos and patches are placed on: chest, shoulder, belt, wrist. Not on the face.
- Fabrics read as fabric: solid fills with bold outlines, not gradients

### 5. Technology Serves
- HUDs, scanners, wrist devices, companion robots — present, helpful, visible
- No tech as threat or surveillance. Tech is a tool someone chose to carry.
- Robot companions (especially dogs) are welcome. They count as community.

### 6. Community Signals
- Flags, patches, badges — the SolarPunk community is legible from the outside
- Poses that show engagement: leaning, looking up, kneeling to a plant, reading, checking a wrist device
- Avoid isolated dramatic poses. Prefer: "person in the middle of their day."

### 7. Lighting: Top-Left, Consistent
- Light source is always top-left
- Highlights on top-facing surfaces (shoulders, top of head, tops of boxes)
- Shadows fall bottom-right
- This is not optional — it is how the isometric grid communicates form

## Visual Boundaries (DON'T)

- **Don't** use dystopian color temperature (deep blacks, blood reds as base) — this is not that future
- **Don't** use smooth gradients — pixel art means discrete color steps with bold outlines
- **Don't** put masks, helmets, or full-face coverage on characters — the face is the identity
- **Don't** create characters in isolation — someone always has something: a companion, a plant, a tool, a flag
- **Don't** mix incompatible genre signals — this is not cyberpunk noir, not post-apocalyptic, not grimdark. If there's a skull motif, it reads as guardian, not menace
- **Don't** exceed **16 colors** per composition — hard cap, enforced by discipline
- **Don't** generate at sub-512px resolution or non-square aspect ratios — the grid needs room
- **Don't** use anti-aliasing or blur — pixel edges are clean. This is not photographic style transfer

## Jimmi — The Legend

Jimmi is the single character who appears across all classes and ages. He is the connective tissue of the SolarPunks story.

**Jimmi's Rule:** Jimmi does not shine. Yet he is the light.

| Variant | Rarity | Age | Distinguishing Feature |
|---------|--------|-----|----------------------|
| Baby Jimmi | Most Rare | Infant | Wide eyes, full hair, carried |
| Young Jimmi | Ultra Rare | Adult in suit | Successful, clean, composed |
| Adult Jimmi | Rare | Prime | Comfortable, working, grounded |
| Ole Jimmi | Rare | Elderly | Weathered, toothless, calm |
| Jimmi Tombstone | 1 of 1 | All | Final form — no shine, all light |

**Visual rule for Jimmi:** He is always wearing something from the community — a class badge, a SolarPunk flag patch, something that says he belonged. He is never alone in the frame.

## Character Composition Rules

### Proportions (15px base grid)
```
Head:   5-6px  ████  (33-40%)
Torso:  4-5px  ████  (27-33%)
Legs:   3-4px  ███   (20-27%)
Arms:   3px    ███   (20%)
```

### Character at Rest (Standard Pose)
- Standing, weight slightly forward
- Hands visible, relaxed — not fists, not weapons unless Skulls class
- Facing: 3/4 view, facing right (EBOY default)

### Permitted Poses by Class
- **Tech:** Checking wrist device, holding tool, fist-bumping AI
- **Bio:** Kneeling to plant, reading air, hands in soil
- **Wildlife:** Kneeling to companion animal, hand extended
- **Musical:** Headphone-adjusted, one hand raised in rhythm
- **Achievement:** Reading, writing, looking at badge
- **Skulls:** Alert stance, shield held, scanning
- **Explorer:** Map-reading, looking up, gear-check
- **Fiscal:** Counting, ledger-checking, confident lean

### Prohibited Poses
- Aggressive or combat-ready (except Skulls — alert is fine, violent is not)
- Isolated dramatic close-up (no character is alone in the composition)
- Defensive crouching (unless the character is in danger — they are never in danger)

## Technical Specs

### For p5.js Generation (SB26 Generator)
- Canvas: **512×512**
- Pixel scale: **3** (renders 15px character as ~45px on canvas — clean, not blurry)
- Color mode: **HSB** (360, 100, 100) — use this, not RGB
- Color cap: **16 per composition**
- Background: **warm off-white** — HSB(40, 15, 98) — not pure white, not grey
- Lighting: top-left, baked into fills — no dynamic lighting

### For AI Image Generation (Fal.ai / Reference)
**Use FLUX.1-dev** for final renders, FLUX.1-schnell for iterations.

```
Prompt structure (in order of importance):
1. Solar Isometric style — EBOY pixel art — TiliX 2:1 grid — 15px character
2. [Class name] character — [body type] — [clothing description]
3. [Pose] — [companion/element if any]
4. Privacy Agent rule: face visible, no mask, no helmet
5. Color palette: primary [hex/name], secondary [hex/name], accent [hex/name]
6. Top-left lighting — bold outlines — limited palette (16 colors max)
7. Background: transparent or warm gradient
```

**Parameters:**
- num_inference_steps: 28-35
- guidance_scale: 3.5-5.0 (lower = sharper, less smoothing)
- image_size: 512×512 or 1024×1024
- output_format: png
- seed: fixed for character consistency across renders

## DO/DON'T Summary

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| Align to TiliX 7×7 grid | Wing the proportions |
| Use warm-dominant palette | Base in cool colors |
| Show the face | Cover the face |
| Include a companion or community element | Isolate the character |
| Top-left lighting | Dynamic or multi-source lighting |
| 16 colors max | Gradient fills or anti-aliasing |
| Functional clothing | Fantasy formal wear |
| Technology serves | Technology threatens |
| Maintain the community | Create lone heroes |
| "Person in their day" pose | Dramatic action pose |
| Jimmi wears community signals | Jimmi appears unmarked |

## Reference Touchstones

- **EBOY** — the definitive isometric pixel art standard. Their grid is this style's grid.
- **BOZGO** — hand-inked anime linework translated to pixel scale. Use for face and hand clarity.
- **Japanese isometric street scenes** (warm afternoon light, brick, greenery, utility) — the world the characters inhabit.
- **Warm Solarpunk Pinterest collection** — see `assets/palettes/` for the curated reference board.
